Who Owns Your Research: A Survey

Jan Vitek

March 12, 2015

There were 819 responses with 54% of respondents being members of the ACM. Membership is higher in North America (74%) and lower in the rest of the world (49%). The majority of respondents is from Europe (52%) with the other biggest chunk being from the USA and Canada (37%). The geographic distribution is shown in the following pie chart.

The respondent are split between academia (79%) and industry (19%). Lastly, the relative seniority of respondents as measured by the time from their first publication is summarized in the following pie chart.

In the rest of this document we will use the following shortcuts: USA to mean responses from North America, EU for responses that come from Europe, Senior for respondent who have published their first paper more than 20 years ago, Middle for those who have first published ten years ago, and Junior for authors who have published less than 10 years ago. ACM will denote respondent who are members of the ACM.

On the importance of Open Access

On the question asking if open access (OA) was a moral imperative the vast majority of respondents agreed (73%). The other responses where a a desirable features and not essential. The proportion of respondents who believe that adopting OA is a necessity is different depending on origin and seniority. The following table shows the most significant differences. Respondents outside of the USA were slightly more favorable to OA (75%). Seniority seemed to play a big role in attitudes, with more junior respondents having stronger feelings (81%). The most striking difference is between Senior USA Academics and Junior USA Academic the former favoring OA at 53% and the latter at a whopping 87%.

In all groups the majority views open access as a moral imperative.
